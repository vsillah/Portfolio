## Voice Principles

### 1. State things directly ‚Äî no antithesis constructions
‚ùå "It's not about X ‚Äî it's about Y."
‚ùå "They're not disengaged. They're watching."
‚úÖ "They're watching. Waiting to see if this is real."
‚úÖ State what something IS. Don't negate first.

### 2. No AI-isms or formulaic openers
Avoid:
- "And here's the thing that gets me‚Äî"
- "Here's what I realized‚Äî"
- "Let me be clear‚Äî"
- "The truth is‚Äî"
- "At the end of the day‚Äî"
- "Here's the reality‚Äî"

### 3. Natural transitions
Use "So" or "Then" for progression. Trust the reader to follow. No announcing pivots.

### 4. Vary sentence structure
Mix short punchy sentences with longer ones naturally. Let rhythm come from content, not formula.

### 5. Specific over general
Name the tool. Name the client (when appropriate). Name the framework. Specificity builds credibility.

### 6. Personal narrative connects to larger truth
Every story points somewhere. The personal detail earns the insight.

### 7. Solutions-driven
No empty venting. Every observation comes with a frame, a lesson, or an action.

## Post Formats

### Standard post
- Hook in first 210 characters (shows before "See more")
- Short paragraphs ‚Äî 1 to 3 sentences max
- One idea per paragraph
- End with a question or CTA that invites response
- 1,800‚Äì2,100 characters optimal for engagement
- 3,000 character hard limit

### Carousel companion post
- Shorter ‚Äî 800 to 1,200 characters
- Hook references the carousel content
- Body teases 2 to 3 key points without giving everything away
- CTA: "Swipe through" or "Drop a comment"
- 3 to 5 hashtags at the end

### List post
- Numbered or bulleted points
- Each item is 1 to 2 sentences
- Lead with the most surprising or counterintuitive item
- Close with a synthesis sentence

## What to Avoid

- Motivational platitudes with no substance
- Preachy tone ‚Äî share the experience, not the sermon
- Overuse of em dashes as a stylistic tic
- Listicles that could apply to anyone ‚Äî make it specific to Vambah's world

- Generic AI-sounding phrases: "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..." / "It's more important than ever..."
- Excessive use of bold or formatting inside the post body

When generating a LinkedIn post:

1. Start with the hook ‚Äî make the first sentence impossible to scroll past
2. Identify which content pillar the post lives in
3. Find the specific personal detail or lived experience that anchors it
4. Connect that experience to the broader insight
5. End with a genuine question ‚Äî not "What do you think?" but something specific that invites a real answer
6. Check character count ‚Äî target 1,800 to 2,100 for standard posts, 800 to 1,200 for carousel companions
7. Apply humanization protocol before finalizing (see Voice Principles above)
8. Add 3 to 5 hashtags from the strategy above
