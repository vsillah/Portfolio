# Codex Review Receipt

Subject: `drafts/agentified-expanded-outline-fable5-v1.md`

Overall recommendation: `revise`

Fable 5 produced strong raw material, especially around receipts, source safety, memory, controller logic, evaluation, human review, AutoResearch, and the final "Agentified Day." It is not ready as the canonical outline yet because it drifts from the 26-chapter spine in the opening act map, invents details, overclaims outcomes, and occasionally sounds more like a generic agent startup book than Vambah's product-management sequel to `Accelerated`.

Minimum bar was not met because sequel fit, structure, voice, and Portfolio proof need revision.

## Top Required Revisions

1. Restore the canonical 26-chapter architecture from `agentified-book-blueprint.md`. The Fable book-spine chunk proposes a 12-chapter architecture that conflicts with the actual outline.
2. Remove `Sam Okafor`. Use `Sam` only unless Vambah explicitly approves a full name.
3. Replace invented outcome claims with structural proof. Remove or soften lines like "renewal signs Wednesday," "ships before boarding," "board meeting shortens by twenty minutes," "duplicate work drops to near zero," and "ships in eleven days."
4. Correct Portfolio proof language. Use `Portfolio`, `Agent Ops`, `Open Brain`, `Shaka`, `Mission Control`, `agent_handoffs`, work items, approvals, cost events, and governance exports. Avoid "Agentified reference repo" and invented table names unless they exist.
5. Keep spend execution gated. Chapter 1 and Chapter 17 need to avoid implying agents directly move money, shift ad budget, or auto-approve refunds without explicit payment/spend authority.
6. Tighten agent-role accuracy. In Mobile Foundry, Amina is Trend Strategist, Imhotep is Prototype Architect, Kandake is Commercialization Captain, and Shaka routes approvals. Do not make Amina a generic review agent or Kandake the governance layer.
7. Replace generic reader exercises that assume everyone has `/admin/agents` with portable versions: "open your equivalent cockpit/control surface."
8. Remove or reframe "fully autonomous" language except as a rejected claim. The book's stance is governed capacity, not autonomy theater.
9. Preserve Vambah's moral/practical voice by adding more operational reality and less venture-style phrasing like "the moat" when it starts to sound generic.
10. Add a clean "Structural Enhancements Over Accelerated" section that is tied to craft: clearer act turns, recurring receipt motif, stronger artifact ladder, proof-before-trust close, and explicit refusal as climax.

## Voice And Language Notes

Strong lines to preserve:

- "An agent without a receipt is a rumor."
- "The bug isn't in either agent. The bug is between them."
- "Human review is authority, not slowdown."
- "Slack is the doorbell; Mission Control is the house."
- "The upgrade that matters is the gate, the memory, and the review."

Language to soften or remove:

- "the moat" when used generically.
- "autonomous business."
- "fully autonomous production."
- Any startup-style claim that implies agent superiority over human judgment.

## Privacy And Source-Safety Notes

- No raw private content appears, but several fictional client scenes are specific enough that prose should keep them clearly composite.
- Governance exports must be described as review evidence and advisory proof, not certification.
- Client-safe export examples should not include raw packet contents.
- Portfolio proof should not expose private traces, prompts, secrets, client records, or raw approval payloads.
