# Fable 5 first-draft generation brief

Use this brief when drafting `Agentified` prose with Claude Code agent `Fable 5`.

## Objective

Create the first complete manuscript draft of `Agentified`, a sequel to `Accelerated`.

This is a first draft, not final prose. It should be readable, structurally complete, and voice-aligned enough for a later revision pass.

## Book thesis

`Accelerated` taught product teams to build systems that learn. `Agentified` begins when that learning system can start doing work.

The new problem is authority.

Anyone can build an agent demo. The advantage belongs to builders who can turn agent output into governed work: remembered, routed, evaluated, approved, reversible, and traceable.

## Voice

Write in Vambah's register:

- grounded, reflective, practical, morally clear, and systems-minded
- scene first, system diagnosis second, practical framework third
- plain strong sentences
- human stakes tied to operational reality
- no generic AI hype
- no detached consulting language
- no fake metrics, fake client wins, or unsupported outcomes
- no raw private logs, private chats, secrets, phone numbers, addresses, or account details

The book should feel like a bridge between survival-shaped communities and technology-shaped futures. Innovation should feel human, moral, and implementable.

## Story rules

- The protagonist is `Sam`. Do not invent a surname unless explicitly approved later.
- Sam returns from `Accelerated` with a product learning system already in motion.
- The next tension is agent authority: agents can draft, route, summarize, recommend, evaluate, and prepare work, but the organization needs receipts, gates, memory rules, approval rules, and rollback paths.
- The story should be told through grounded product and operating scenes, not science-fiction spectacle.
- Every chapter should move Sam from confidence, tension, or discomfort into a clearer operating principle.

## Safety rules

- Agents may prepare, draft, recommend, route, evaluate, summarize, and package evidence.
- Humans approve publishing, spending, memory promotion, client claims, irreversible action, and external sends.
- Treat human review as the trust layer, not a nuisance.
- Treat source safety as a moral and operational discipline.
- Keep Portfolio proof structural and public-safe: trace records, approval gates, Mission Control, Open Brain records, Agent Inbox, Agent Kanban, Mobile App Foundry roles, AutoResearch gates, and governance exports.
- Do not imply Portfolio is a finished commercial product unless the source chapter says so.

## Style restrictions

Avoid:

- "fully autonomous"
- "autonomous business"
- "not just / not only" constructions
- "landscape"
- "unlock"
- "seamless"
- "robust"
- "revolutionary"
- "pivotal"
- "crucial"
- "delve"
- "vibrant"
- "tapestry"
- "showcase"
- "underscores"
- "testament"
- generic rule-of-three padding
- em dashes
- AI assistant phrases like "here is" or "let's dive in"

Use straight quotes and ASCII punctuation.

## Chapter shape

Each chapter should include:

1. A concrete opening scene.
2. Sam's internal or product-management tension.
3. The upgraded `Accelerated` principle.
4. The agentic operating-system concept.
5. A practical framework or tool the reader can use.
6. A grounded example connected to Portfolio-style proof.
7. A reader exercise or operating question.
8. A closing principle that is crisp enough to quote.

Do not force these into visible template headers unless it helps the chapter. It should read like a book, not a worksheet.

## Length

Target 1,200 to 1,800 words per chapter for this first draft.

The critical safety note can be shorter: 700 to 1,100 words.
