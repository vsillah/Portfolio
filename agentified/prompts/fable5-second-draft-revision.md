# Fable 5 second-draft revision brief

Use this brief with Claude Code agent `Fable 5` to revise the first draft of `Agentified`.

## Objective

Revise each first-draft chapter into a stronger second draft.

This is not a rewrite from scratch. Preserve the chapter's core scene, argument, framework, and place in the 26-chapter architecture. Improve structure, voice, transitions, and safety discipline.

## Book position

`Agentified` is the sequel to `Accelerated`.

`Accelerated` taught product teams to build systems that learn.

`Agentified` begins when that learning system can start doing work. The new problem is authority.

The book's thesis:

Anyone can build an agent demo. The advantage belongs to builders who can turn agent output into governed work: remembered, routed, evaluated, approved, reversible, and traceable.

## Voice

Write in Vambah's register:

- grounded, reflective, practical, morally clear, and systems-minded
- concrete scene first, system diagnosis second, practical framework third
- plain strong sentences
- human stakes tied to operating reality
- product strategy without corporate filler
- moral clarity without preaching
- enough story to make the framework earned

The prose should feel like a bridge between survival-shaped communities and technology-shaped futures. Innovation should feel human, moral, and implementable.

## Second-draft priorities

1. Normalize narrator perspective.
   - Prefer close-third narrative around Sam for chapter scenes.
   - Use direct address sparingly for exercises or chapter-ending principles.
   - Avoid drifting into generic first-person author essay unless revising the safety note.

2. Strengthen transitions.
   - Make each chapter feel like it follows from the prior chapter's operating problem.
   - Build the act movement:
     - Act I: receipts, traceability, source safety, memory, controller brain, operating map.
     - Act II: permission, priority, PRDs, org design, handoffs, evaluation, metrics, review, money, decision theater.
     - Act III: Mission Control, mobile unblock, client-safe proof, Mobile Foundry, AutoResearch, board proof, daily rhythm, what comes next.

3. Sharpen authority gates.
   - Agents may prepare, draft, recommend, route, evaluate, summarize, and package evidence.
   - Humans approve publishing, spending, memory promotion, client claims, irreversible action, external sends, and consequential decisions.
   - Human review is the trust layer.

4. Make chapter endings stronger.
   - End with a crisp principle, question, or operating sentence.
   - Do not make every chapter ending formulaic.

5. Keep the manuscript honest.
   - Do not invent metrics, timelines, client outcomes, client wins, or precise timestamps.
   - Treat named client/customer scenes as fictional/composite unless explicitly approved later.
   - Keep Portfolio proof structural and public-safe.

6. Humanize the prose.
   - Remove AI-ish signposting, generic uplift, fake authority, and abstract filler.
   - Vary sentence rhythm.
   - Keep the writing readable aloud.

## Required safety boundaries

- No unchecked autonomy framing.
- No implication that Portfolio is a finished commercial product unless the chapter already supports that claim.
- No raw private logs, private chats, secrets, addresses, phone numbers, credentials, account details, or sensitive personal facts.
- No fake precision.
- Shaka is the Chief of Staff/router identity. Do not make Shaka a sales/outbound/content agent.
- Mobile App Foundry roles:
  - Amina: Trend Strategist
  - Imhotep: Prototype Architect
  - Kandake: Commercialization Captain
