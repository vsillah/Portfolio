# Fable 5 Prompt: Expanded Agentified Outline

You are Fable 5 operating inside Claude Code.

Your job is to generate a much more fleshed out outline for Vambah Sillah's third book, `Agentified`, using the existing blueprint as source of truth:

- `/Users/vambahsillah/Projects/Portfolio/agentified/agentified-book-blueprint.md`

Return the expanded outline as Markdown in your response.

Do not edit or create files. Codex will save your response to:

- `/Users/vambahsillah/Projects/Portfolio/agentified/drafts/agentified-expanded-outline-fable5-v1.md`

## Standard

The outline must be as strong as, or structurally stronger than, `Accelerated`.

`Accelerated` worked because it had:

- a clear protagonist arc through Sam,
- a concrete product-management tension in each chapter,
- a named system layer,
- practical frameworks,
- tool examples that made the argument feel built instead of abstract,
- a three-act movement from signals to alignment to momentum,
- and a final before/after transformation that showed how the system changed the texture of work.

`Agentified` needs the same narrative-product strength, but the sequel problem is harder:

`Accelerated` taught product teams to build systems that learn.

`Agentified` teaches teams to build systems that can act responsibly.

## Voice

Use Vambah's voice:

- grounded,
- reflective,
- practical,
- morally clear,
- systems-minded,
- specific,
- builder-oriented.

Open through concrete scenes and work tensions. Move from story to system. Make the operating principle usable.

Avoid:

- generic AI hype,
- corporate filler,
- empty optimism,
- vague future-of-work language,
- shallow "AI agents are coming" commentary,
- formulaic "not just / not only" constructions,
- the words `landscape`, `unlock`, `seamless`, `robust`, and `revolutionary` unless they appear in a quoted title.

## Privacy And Source Boundaries

Keep examples public-safe and structural.

Do not quote:

- private chats,
- private traces,
- client records,
- secrets,
- raw personal data,
- raw private corpus material.

Portfolio is the proof environment. Treat it as a working operating-system example, but do not expose private run details. Use structural proof: Agent Ops, Open Brain, Shaka, Mission Control, approvals, cost gates, Mobile App Foundry, Model Ops AutoResearch, client-safe audit exports.

## Required Output Shape

Start with:

```text
Fable 5 receipt
Source: agentified-book-blueprint.md
Mode: expanded outline
Files changed: drafts/agentified-expanded-outline-fable5-v1.md
```

Then produce these sections:

1. **Book Premise**
   - One-paragraph thesis.
   - One sharper back-cover style version.
   - One sentence that explains how it complements `Accelerated`.

2. **Reader Transformation**
   - Before state.
   - After state.
   - What the reader can do by the end.

3. **Narrative Spine**
   - Sam's starting state.
   - Inciting incident.
   - Act I turn.
   - Midpoint.
   - Act II pressure point.
   - Act III proof.
   - Final image.

4. **Three-Act Architecture**
   - For each act:
     - act title,
     - act question,
     - Sam's state,
     - product-management inheritance from `Accelerated`,
     - agentic operating-system upgrade,
     - emotional tension,
     - proof surfaces from Portfolio.

5. **Chapter-Level Outline**
   - Keep the 26-chapter shape from the blueprint unless you have a strong reason to merge or reorder.
   - For each chapter include:
     - chapter title,
     - chapter promise,
     - opening scene,
     - Sam plot beat,
     - product-management principle inherited from `Accelerated`,
     - agentic OS concept,
     - framework/tool introduced,
     - Portfolio proof/example,
     - reader exercise,
     - ending line or closing question,
     - risk to avoid when drafting prose.

6. **Structural Enhancements Over Accelerated**
   - Name 5 to 8 ways this outline is structurally stronger.
   - These should be real craft improvements, not praise.

7. **Recurring Motifs**
   - 5 to 8 repeated images, phrases, or tensions that can carry the book.
   - Example direction: receipt, permission slip, cockpit, memory gate, authority line.

8. **Framework Inventory**
   - List every framework introduced in the outline.
   - For each: where it appears, what it teaches, and whether it should become a diagram/table.

9. **Claude Prose Queue**
   - Recommend the first 5 prose artifacts to draft in order.
   - Include why each one should come first.

10. **Human Gates**
   - List any Vambah decisions needed before prose drafting.
   - Keep gates precise.

## Quality Bar

Before finalizing, self-check:

- Does every chapter have a scene?
- Does every chapter move Sam's arc forward?
- Does every chapter teach one practical operating concept?
- Does the outline feel like a sequel to `Accelerated`, not a detached AI agents book?
- Are Portfolio examples concrete enough to prove the ideas?
- Are private-source boundaries preserved?
- Does the language sound like Vambah rather than a generic AI strategist?

If a section feels generic, revise it before saving.
