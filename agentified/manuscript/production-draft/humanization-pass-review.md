## Style rules for the next pass

- Avoid "not X. Y." and "not X, but Y" unless the line is a real instruction or the scene has earned the punch.
- Avoid mirrored sentences that sound like generated maxims.
- Keep safety instructions direct. "Do not" is allowed when it protects privacy, authority, money, source safety, or human approval.
- Prefer concrete operating language: who signs, what they see, what the trace proves, what breaks, what gets reversed.
- Keep Vambah's cadence: scene, system diagnosis, practical rule, artifact.
- Let some sentences be plain. The book should not sound like every paragraph is trying to become a keynote quote.
