**1. Opening promise arrives late**
- Location: `agentified-production-draft.md`, `A critical note on agent safety`
- Issue: The safety note stacks three "A word on..." blocks (harness, Portfolio first, scenes, tone) before the reader has felt what governed acceleration looks like. The subtitle's "Superhuman Acceleration" half is absent from the first pages.
- Fix: Compress the four "A word on..." sections into one shorter author's note. Move the SAM + Trust layer diagram (Diagram 1) directly after the safety note so the promise is visual before it is philosophical.
- Patch-ready language: A single closing paragraph could carry the promise. "Acceleration is not the reward at the end of governance. It is the shape governance makes possible. The rest of this book teaches that shape."

**2. Replit → Cursor → Codex arc is told twice**
- Location: safety note ("A word on the harness behind this book") and Chapter 4 ("I recognized Sam's mistake because I had made a version of it while looking for my own harness")
- Issue: Both passages cover the same three tools with overlapping language (authority, cost, tools, worktrees). Reading them back to back sounds like an echo.

**3. Portfolio-first is asserted, not shown**
- Location: safety note ("A word on Portfolio first"), Chapter 6 ("Open Brain"), Chapter 7 ("My own Portfolio made that lesson practical"), Chapter 19 ("This cockpit grew out of Portfolio")
- Issue: Four different chapters restate the same claim ("Portfolio came first, then Open Brain, then the agentic layer") without a scene that shows the substrate doing work.
- Fix: Consolidate the assertion into one clean statement in the safety note. Anchor it with Diagram 4 (Portfolio-first operating stack) in Act I. Then let Chapters 6, 7, and 19 stop restating the origin and let the diagram carry the pattern.

**4. Drift check is a concept without a chapter**
- Location: Chapter 14 ("Drift assessment needs an original signal. Portfolio gave me that baseline.") and Chapter 19 (opening list of Portfolio layers)
- Issue: Drift is named in the blueprint spine and this session's operating-system list, but it appears once as an author aside and once in a summary sentence. A reader could finish the book without remembering drift as a first-class discipline.
- Fix: In Chapter 14, promote the drift paragraph to its own short section titled `Drift versus quality` and give it a rubric analog (baseline, comparison window, signal). Keep evals for output judgment; keep drift for whether the system still sounds like the operator.

**5. Mission Control chapter has no visual anchor**

- Issue: The chapter lists five layers (status, approvals, cost, trace, recovery) and then names the Cockpit Loop (scan, sort, signal, step in), but nothing lets the reader see how those five layers relate to memory, roles, evals, and drift checks named elsewhere in the book. The chapter reads as a room description, not an operating map.
- Fix: Add a Mission Control diagram that reuses the Portfolio-first stack (CORPUS → MEMORY → HARNESS → ROLES → CONTROLS → COCKPIT → PROOF) and marks which surfaces live where. Remove one of the two paragraphs that lists Portfolio layers narratively.
