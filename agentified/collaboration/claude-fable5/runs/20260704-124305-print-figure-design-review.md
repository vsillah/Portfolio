Fable 5 print figure receipt

**1. Grayscale reproduction is the biggest print risk.** The visual grammar leans on three semantic colors: gold accent, teal for verified proof, red for unresolved risk. In a black-only interior (typical for trade non-fiction), teal and red collapse to nearly identical mid-grays and the gold rule around the paper inset flattens. Rework the figures so meaning survives grayscale: pair each color with a shape or fill pattern (solid card for verified, hatched card for risk, thin outline for source) and test each figure at 100% grayscale before signing off.

**2. Body text inside cards is under the print minimum.** Card subtitle lines render at 18–19px on a 1600px SVG canvas. Scaled to a 4.5–5.5" print measure at 300 DPI, that lands around 6–7 pt — below the 8–9 pt floor for readable body text in a physical book. Raise all secondary card text to at least 22px in source and shorten labels so nothing wraps at print size. Verify by exporting a single figure at final print dimensions and reading it at arm's length.

**3. Figure numbering and captioning need to move out of the manuscript H3 hierarchy.** Right now each figure is introduced as `### Figure 0.1: ...` — an H3 heading, then the image, then an unlabeled paragraph that acts as caption. For print, the number and title belong inside a caption block below (or above) the image, styled distinctly from body prose (small caps or italic sans, "Figure 0.1." lockup), not as a chapter subsection heading. The workbook A.M.I.N.A. figure currently has no figure number at all — give it a workbook-scoped label like `Figure W.1` and add it to the front-matter figure list.

**4. Numbering scheme should be declared once, up front.** The book mixes Arabic (`0.1`, `1.1`, `8.1`) with Roman for act openers (`II.1`) and an unnumbered workbook figure. That mix is defensible, but readers need a Figures list in the front matter that spells out the convention: `0.x` for front matter, `[chapter].x` for chapters, `[Act].x` for act openers, `W.x` for workbook.

**5. The figures still feel like dashboard components, not book illustrations.** The drop shadows on every card, the colored band across the top of each card, and the rounded-rectangle grid are borrowed patterns from Notion/Linear/admin UIs. That reads as "app screenshot" in a printed book. For the print edition, flatten the shadows, replace the top color band with a thin rule or a small numeric marker, and let typographic weight carry hierarchy. Keep the shadowed/colored version for the digital workbook; do not use it as the print master.

**6. Figure 8.1 (Portfolio-first operating stack) will fight the page.** Seven vertically stacked nodes in a 6×9 trim size become either a narrow column that wastes horizontal space or a full-page figure that breaks reading flow. Consider a two-column layered stack (Corpus + Memory on the substrate row, Harness + Roles + Controls in the middle, Cockpit + Proof on top) so the figure reads at half-page height. This also reinforces the "operating stack" metaphor better than a single-file chain.

**7. Figure II.1 (Authority ladder) needs the gates re-integrated.** The five rungs (Prepare → Recommend → Route → Stage → Execute) plus four dashed gate callouts (Receipt required, Source check required, Human gate required, Rollback path required) create six visual objects competing for attention. In grayscale the dashed lines lose their distinction from solid arrows. Move each gate condition inline as a small badge on its rung, not as a dashed satellite node.

**8. Amina should appear as a named guide inside at least one figure.** She is the operating discipline made visible in prose but is absent from every diagram. The A.M.I.N.A. inside SAM workbook figure is the natural place: add a small persona mark or "Amina notes:" side rail with one line per stage. Right now the figure treats A.M.I.N.A. as an acronym loop, not as a guide.

**Format sufficiency.** PNG + SVG is sufficient for editorial review and the digital workbook, but it is **not** sufficient for print production. Before handing off to an interior designer or POD vendor, add: (a) 600 DPI grayscale TIFF or high-resolution PDF exports with embedded fonts, generated from the SVG sources; (b) at least one full-size proof of each figure at final print dimensions to check text legibility; (c) EPS as a nice-to-have if the print vendor asks. Update `scripts/render-diagram-assets.mjs` to emit PDF alongside PNG and SVG so the print masters travel with the sources. SVG alone should not go to the printer.
